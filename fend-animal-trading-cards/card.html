Animal's name

Alpacas don't have teeth in the top-front of their mouths. This gives them the appearance of having an underbite.

  • Size: They are 4 to 7 feet (120 to 225 cm) long and weigh 121 to 143 lbs. (55 to 65 kilograms)
  • Habitat: Alpacas are native to the Andes, at elevations of up to 15,750 feet (4,800 meters).
  • Diet: As herbivores, alpacas only eat vegetation.
  • Offspring: Alpacas breed once a year, and as livestock they are often induced to breed at any time.

Adorable, docile and soft, alpacas are prized as pets and cattle around the world. There are no wild alpacas.
